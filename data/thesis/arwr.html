Platform Technology: TRiM

Technology Overview

Arrowhead's Targeted RNAi Molecule (TRiM) platform enables tissue-specific delivery of siRNA therapeutics. The platform uses proprietary ligand-targeting chemistry to direct RNAi payloads to specific cell types.

Tissue Targeting Capabilities

  • Liver (GalNAc) - Most advanced, multiple programs
  • Lung (inhaled) - ARO-RAGE, ARO-MUC5AC
  • Muscle - ARO-DUX4 (FSHD), ARO-DM1
  • CNS - ARO-MAPT (Alzheimer's), ARO-ATXN2

Plozasiran (ARO-APOC3)

TargetAPOC3
StageApproved (FCS) / Phase 3 (sHTG)
IndicationFCS, Severe Hypertriglyceridemia, Mixed Hyperlipidemia
ModalityRNAi (siRNA)
DosingSubcutaneous injection every 12 weeks (quarterly)

Mechanism

Silences APOC3 mRNA in hepatocytes. APOC3 inhibits lipoprotein lipase. By reducing APOC3, plozasiran increases LPL activity, accelerating clearance of triglyceride-rich lipoproteins.

FDA Approved

Investment View
Bull Thesis

First-in-class RNAi therapeutic for APOC3 with best-in-class 80% triglyceride reduction and durable quarterly dosing. FCS approval validates mechanism. sHTG represents 10x larger market (~500K US patients). SHASTA-3/4/5 trials ongoing for broader indications. Amgen partnership provides experienced CV sales force.

Bear Thesis

FCS is tiny orphan market (~3,000 US patients). sHTG faces competition from Ionis olezarsen. Amgen controls commercialization meaning majority economics flow to partner. No CV outcomes data yet.

Key Question

Will Phase 3 sHTG trials (SHASTA-3/4/5) succeed and enable broad commercial launch in the much larger sHTG population?

TargetANGPTL3

ModalityRNAi (siRNA)

DrugCompanyMechanismStageEfficacyDifferentiation
Evinacumab (Evkeeza) Regeneron ANGPTL3 antibody Approved (HoFH) 47% LDL-C reduction Zodasiran shows higher efficacy (65% vs 47%), quarterly SC vs monthly IV
Investment View
Bull Thesis

ANGPTL3 is genetically validated - LOF mutations show low LDL, low TGs, reduced CV events. Zodasiran achieves dual LDL-C (65%) and TG (70%) reduction with quarterly dosing. Phase 3 HoFH trial recruiting.

Bear Thesis

Crowded lipid market with statins and PCSK9i. Evinacumab (Regeneron) already approved for HoFH. Quarterly injection vs daily statin limits adoption.

Key Question

Can zodasiran demonstrate CV outcomes benefit to justify premium pricing?

TargetAAT (Alpha-1 Antitrypsin)
StagePhase 3
IndicationAlpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency Liver Disease (AATD-LD)
ModalityRNAi (siRNA)
DosingSubcutaneous Q12W (quarterly)

Silences AAT mRNA, reducing both normal and mutant Z-AAT. Prevents accumulation of toxic aggregates in hepatocytes.

Investment View
Bull Thesis

Fazirsiran addresses root cause of AATD liver disease by silencing mutant Z-AAT that aggregates in hepatocytes. Phase 2 showed 96% reduction with histological fibrosis improvement. No approved therapy exists. Takeda partnership provides $1B+ potential milestones.

Bear Thesis

AATD liver disease is rare (~10-15% of AATD patients). Regulatory path unclear - may require clinical outcomes not just Z-AAT reduction. Takeda controls development.

Key Question

Will Phase 3 SEQUOIA show sufficient fibrosis improvement for FDA approval?

ARO-INHBE

TargetINHBE (Inhibin beta E)
StagePhase 1/2
IndicationObesity, Metabolic Dysfunction
ModalityRNAi (siRNA)
DosingSubcutaneous Q12W (quarterly)

Mechanism

Silences INHBE which forms activin E. Activin E signals through ALK7 to promote fat accumulation. Blocking this shifts energy balance toward fat oxidation.

Clinical Development
NCT IDTrialPhaseStatusResult
NCT06700538 ARO-INHBE Phase 1/2 Phase 1/2 Recruiting -6 to -9%
Competitive Landscape
DrugCompanyMechanismStageEfficacyDifferentiation
Semaglutide (Wegovy) Novo Nordisk GLP-1 agonist Approved 15-17% weight loss ARO-INHBE is quarterly vs weekly, no GI side effects
Tirzepatide (Zepbound) Eli Lilly GLP-1/GIP dual agonist Approved 20-25% weight loss Tirzepatide best-in-class efficacy
Investment View
Bull Thesis

Novel genetically-validated obesity target. INHBE LOF mutations associated with lower BMI. Early data shows 6-9% weight loss with quarterly dosing and no GI side effects (unlike GLP-1s). ARWR retains full rights.

Bear Thesis

GLP-1s achieving 15-25% weight loss are becoming standard of care. 6-9% may not be competitive. Mechanism less understood than GLP-1.

Key Question

Can ARO-INHBE achieve >15% weight loss to compete with GLP-1 agonists?
